## The Founding-Member Offer

### The name: "Founding 30"

Simple, countable, credible. Thirty seats, numbered. Every founding member gets a visible badge in the app: **Founding 30 · #07**. (Build the badge in [APP] before seat #1 is sold — Offer Post 3's real-screen shot records it, and this campaign never fakes an app screen.) The number never changes hands — if seat #07 leaves, seat #07 retires with them. (Alternate BM flavour if you prefer: *Perintis 30*. Pick one and never rename it.)

### What's inside (say all four, every time)

1. **RM49/month, locked for life.** Not "first year". As long as your subscription stays active, the price never moves — even when the public price is RM79. *(Recommended pricing — adjust to your economics.)*
2. **Done-for-you book setup.** The real reason agents never start a CRM is keying in 500 clients. Founding members skip it: export your contacts (Excel, Google Contacts, phone export — whatever you have) and WhatsApp the file over. Your book is live inside [APP] **before the first call** — names, notes, birthdays, renewal dates: whatever your file actually holds. (A bare phone export carries names and numbers only — say so in the DM, and fill in policies and renewal dates together on the first call.) You wake up to a working system, not homework.
   - **PDPA line (say it unprompted):** "Your file is used for your import only. It goes into *your* account, nobody else's — not your agency, not other members, not me after setup. Deleted from my side once your book is live. Export or delete everything anytime."
3. **WhatsApp support, direct with the founder.** Not a ticket system, not a chatbot. The same number that DMs the demo is the number that answers when something confuses you. Founding members are the people who shape the roadmap — they ask, features appear.
4. **The founding badge + first look.** Every new feature ships to the Founding 30 first. Their feedback decides what stays.

### The deadline mechanic — a real count, never a timer

- The only scarcity in this campaign is the **seat count, publicly tracked**: a pinned comment / pinned post that reads "**[X] of 30 founding seats left**", updated the day a seat fills.
- No countdown clocks. No "offer ends tonight". No resetting deadlines. If seats fill slowly, the number just sits there — that stillness *is* the honesty, and the audience will notice.
- When seat 30 fills: post it once ("Founding 30 is closed. Standard is RM79 — still worth it."), close it forever, and never reopen it. The lock is what makes the next offer believable.

### Risk reversal (remove every reason to hesitate)

- **14-day full trial. No card.** Everything unlocked — reminders, sequences, renewal list, birthday posters, all of it. Nothing to cancel because nothing was charged.
- **Your book is YOURS.** One tap exports everything — clients, notes, policies, history — to Excel, anytime, forever. This is the line that beats the "free company CRM" objection, so say it as a contrast: *"The company CRM is free because the company owns it. Switch agencies and your book stays behind. [APP] is yours — leave anytime and it walks out with you."*
- **Leave clean.** Cancel anytime, export first, and on request the data is deleted. PDPA is not fine print here — it's a selling point to an audience that's scared of data poaching.

### The anchor math — words to say out loud

This is the script for the DM voice note, the live Q&A, the kopitiam conversation. Not a spreadsheet — a story with three beats.

> **Beat 1 — the lapse.**
> "You saw Day 21 — Madam Chen. Six years of premiums, never missed one. Then her card expired, the auto-debit bounced quietly, and nobody was watching. Claim rejected: RM40,000. Her agent didn't lose a claim that day — he lost her, her family, and every person she would ever have referred. All because one red alert didn't exist."
>
> **Beat 2 — the price of watching.**
> "So here's what watching costs. RM79 a month. RM49 if you're one of the Founding 30 — locked for life. That's less than one teh tarik a day. You spend more on parking to *meet* one client than [APP] costs to *keep* all of them."
>
> **Beat 3 — the honest break-even.**
> "And I don't need to promise you the moon. Forget doubling your income — just count ONE. One renewal that didn't slip. One card expiry caught before it lapsed. One buried chat that became an appointment. Any single one of those is a case — and one small case pays for [APP] for the whole year, and then some. Everything after case number one is profit. The math was never the question. The question is only whether you want somebody watching your book with you."

**Internal sizing note (for the founder only — never on screen, never quoted):** a modest medical-card case is roughly RM900–1,200 FYC, versus RM790/year standard or RM588/year founding. That's why "one small case pays for the whole year, and then some" is a safe, honest claim at any tier. Keep the reasoning in your head; keep the numbers off the screen.

## The Team Offer (Unit Managers)

### The package

- **RM39/seat/month for 10+ seats** *(recommended — adjust to your economics)*. Ten seats is RM390/month for the whole unit — say it out loud: "less than one team dinner."
- **One group onboarding session** (90 minutes, Zoom or at the agency office). Each agent sends their own contact export *before* the session, so every book is already live when they walk in. Nobody leaves with homework — everyone leaves with tomorrow morning's follow-up list already waiting.
- Billing flexible: the UM carries it as a unit expense, or splits it per agent — either way it's one group, one price, one onboarding.

### THE feature: team KPI visibility

This is the one thing a UM cannot get anywhere else — not from the company CRM (company-filtered, company-owned), not from screenshots in the group chat, not from chasing "case update pls 🙏" at 9.14pm on the 29th (that's Day 23 — reference it).

- The UM sees every agent's **KPI progress, activity counts, pipeline and renewals at risk** — live, any day of the month. Month-end becomes a glance, not an interrogation.
- Who's drowning shows up on **Tuesday of week one**, not at month-end when it's too late to help. The Day 19 rookie typing her resignation draft — a UM with [APP] sees her forty follow-ups and nine meetings and calls her *before* she sends it.
- **The privacy line that closes agents (say it in front of the team):** "You see the scoreboard, not their chats. Agents keep their own book — client notes and conversations stay theirs. [APP] shows the manager the numbers, not the relationships." This defuses the data-poaching fear on the spot, and it's true.

### The one-line pitch to a UM

> **"One saved rookie pays for the whole unit's year."**

Expansion, if they pause: "You know what it costs to recruit, license and train a rookie who quits at month four — months of your time, gone. Most of them don't quit because they're bad. They quit because they can't see their own progress and you can't see them drowning until month-end. Ten seats cost you RM390 a month. One rookie who stays — just one — covers that many times over. And the seniors get back their evenings."

## Referral Mechanic

### The rule (one sentence, no asterisks)

> **Founding members get one free month for every agent they refer who stays past the 14-day trial and becomes a paying member.**

- **"Stays past trial" is the honest gate.** A referral that signs up and vanishes on day 13 counts for nothing — no gaming, no farm accounts. The free month is applied when the referred agent's first payment goes through.
- **Free months stack.** Refer three agents who stay, get three months free. A founding member who refers twelve agents in a year pays nothing that year — and that is a fine outcome, because those twelve are paying standard price.
- **How it's tracked:** the new agent just mentions the member's name in the SISTEM DM ("Farid intro me"). No codes, no links to break. At this scale, a name is the system.

### Why this mirrors Day 56 (and how to frame it)

Day 56 is Hafiz telling his brother "You very on one 😄" — the referral nobody asked for, earned by six months of never being dropped. The offer works the same way: **don't ask founding members to blast their contacts.** The product earns the mention; the free month is just the thank-you. Frame it exactly like the storyboard framed Hafiz:

> "I'm not asking you to promote anything. But if another agent asks you why your follow-up so fast one — and they will ask — send them my way. Their trial is free, and your next month is on me."

### The forwardable message (give this to every founding member on day one)

> "Eh, you asked about the app I use for follow-ups right? [APP] — founder is Malaysian, you WhatsApp him direct. Comment SISTEM on his page or message him, tell him I intro you. 14 days free, no card one. If you stay, I get a free month, so win-win la 😄"

One emoji, no hard sell, sounds like a person. That's the whole point.

## Demo Production Notes

**Tool:** Jianying (same draft-template workflow as the 60-day batch).

1. **One continuous capture per scene.** Six scenes = six recordings. Never cut inside a scene — the unbroken cursor is the proof this is a real app, not a mockup reel. If you fumble a click, re-record that scene only. Name the files `demo_s1.mp4` … `demo_s6.mp4` so a future UI update means swapping one clip, not rebuilding the video.
2. **Cursor discipline.** Move at half your normal speed. Pause ~0.5s *before* every click so the viewer's eye arrives first. Never circle or wiggle the cursor — the zoom does the pointing, not the mouse.
3. **Capture settings.** Desktop browser at 100% zoom, notifications off, clean tab bar, demo book loaded. Record 1920×1080. If your morning-list screen shows a clock/date, make sure it reads a believable morning time — Scene 1 sells "tomorrow morning."
4. **Zooms (Jianying keyframes).** One zoom per scene, maximum. Scale 100% → 140% over 0.4s, ease-out, hold on the money row, cut to the next scene at 100%. The zoom targets are marked in each scene above.
5. **Vertical delivery.** The demo is watched inside WhatsApp/Messenger on a phone. Build the Jianying project on a 1080×1920 canvas and keyframe-crop the 16:9 capture to follow the action (your zooms do double duty here). Keep all text inside the middle 80% of frame height.
6. **Voiceover.** Record VO *after* picture lock, scene by scene, matching each scene's length. Quiet room, phone mic 20cm away, your own voice — the Malaysian accent is the brand. Target ~140 wpm; if a scene runs long, cut VO words, never speed up the audio.
7. **Music.** One low lo-fi bed under Scenes 1–4 at roughly −20dB, ducked under VO. Kill the music completely at the start of Scene 5 — the honesty beat plays dry. Silence makes it feel true. No music returns until the end-card ping.
8. **Text overlays.** One font family for the whole video (same one as your 60-day overlays). White text, subtle dark backing strip, nothing in the bottom 15% of frame (WhatsApp's UI covers it).
9. **End-card.** Do not rebuild it — append the pre-exported `endcard_YES.mp4` (demo variant, spec below) as the final clip.
10. **Export & delivery.** 1080×1920, 30fps, high bitrate master. Then make two deliverables: (a) a compressed copy under ~16MB that plays natively inside WhatsApp — Malaysian agents press play in-chat, they don't click links; (b) an unlisted hosted link (YouTube/Drive) as backup for the auto-reply DM. Send the native file first, link second.
11. **Re-record trigger.** Any time the app UI changes visibly on a money screen, re-capture that one scene and re-export. The demo must always match what a trial user sees on day one.

| Time | What happens |
|---|---|
| 0.00s | **Hard cut** from content — no fade. All music/VO stops dead at this cut. |
| 0.00–0.15s | Logo scales 92% → 100%, ease-out. |

| 0.40–0.70s | Tagline fades in below the logo. |
| 0.70–2.00s | Hold. Nothing moves. |
| 2.00s | CTA line pops in (no animation longer than 0.1s). |
| 2.00–3.50s | Hold to end. |

### The ping (the audio logo)

- One royalty-free notification "ding/pop," ~0.4s, no reverb tail past 1s. **Not** the actual WhatsApp sound — use a lookalike you have rights to.
- Normalize to −6dBFS. Save the file as `ping.mp3` and never replace it. Same file, every video, forever — in 60 days that sound *is* your brand.
- The 0.25s of silence before it is part of the logo. Don't let music leak across the cut.

### CTA line — two variants only

- **Default (all 60 videos):** `Comment "SISTEM" — I'll DM you the 2-minute demo.` → export as `endcard_SISTEM.mp4`
- **Demo video only:** `Reply YES — send me your contact export.` → export as `endcard_YES.mp4`

Everything else — background, logo, tagline, ping, timing — is pixel-identical between the two. Build the card once in Jianying as a 3.5s draft, duplicate it, change one text line, export both, and treat the two mp4s as frozen brand assets.

# THE DM MACHINE

## The SISTEM Auto-Reply

This fires the moment someone comments **SISTEM** (ManyChat/Chatrace keyword, or manual at small scale). It is three things in one message: a greeting, the demo link, and one question — because a link gets watched or ignored, but a question gets answered. The question is what starts the conversation.

**The message (copy-paste):**

```
Hey [first name] 👋 Saw your SISTEM comment — here's the 2-minute demo:

[DEMO LINK]

Quick one before you watch: you doing personal lines or group ah? Different book, different screens — tell me and I'll point you straight to the part for yours.
```

**Rules:**

- **If manual, reply within 5 minutes during waking hours.** Speed is literally the Day 1 video's lesson — practice what you preach. The lead knows it, too.
- **When they answer the question, drop the script and talk like a person.** Example: *"Personal lines ah — then watch from 0:40, the renewal list. That screen alone is the whole pitch."*
- **Bridge to WhatsApp early.** Malaysian agents live there, not in Messenger: *"Easier on WhatsApp? Msg me here: [wa.me link] — same person, I promise."*
- **Log every commenter into [APP] as a prospect** — pipeline, warm-lead ranking, days-since-contact. You are running your own product on your own leads; when a prospect later asks "you really use this ah?", you show them their own name in your follow-up list.
- If Facebook blocks the DM (privacy settings), reply under their comment: *"DM sent! If never receive, WhatsApp me: [wa.me link]"*

## The 4-Touch Close Sequence

Touch 1 is the auto-reply above. If they reply at any point, you exit the script and have a real conversation — the sequence only resumes (from where it stopped) after 3+ days of silence. If they never reply, the remaining touches go out on this clock:

| Touch | Day | Job |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0 | Demo link + the question |
| 2 | 2 | Re-open with curiosity + the money screenshot |
| 3 | 4 | Surface the two objections they're silently holding |
| 4 | 7 | Founding-seat close + graceful exit |

### Touch 2 — Day 2: "which screen you like?"

```
Hey [name], watched the demo already ah?

Which screen you like most — genuinely curious, different agents pick different ones.
```

**Attach:** the renewal-list screenshot (policies expiring in the next 14 days, red rows visible). Then:

```
This one's my favourite to show — everything expiring in the next 14 days, one list. No more "eh, whose renewal was this month ah?"
```

### Touch 3 — Day 4: surface the objection

```
At this point you're probably wondering 2 things, so let me just answer both:

1. "My agency gives a CRM free what." Ya — and it belongs to the agency. Switch agency, your book stays behind. [APP] is yours: your data, export anytime, goes where you go.

2. "How long to key in 200 clients?" Zero, if you take a founding seat — send me your contact export, I set up your whole book for you, live before your first call.

Which one were you actually thinking? Or something else — ask lah, I answer straight.
```

### Touch 4 — Day 7: the founding-seat close

```
[Name], last message from me, then I stop buzzing you 🙂

Founding member: RM49/month locked for life (standard is RM79) — first 30 agents only, and [X] seats left as I type this. Includes done-for-you setup: send me your contacts, your whole book is live tomorrow.

Want one of the [X]? Reply YES.

If not now, no worries at all — I post daily, just watch how it works. The videos aren't going anywhere.
```

### Timing & STOP rules

- **Send 9am–9pm only.** If a touch day lands odd-hours, it goes next morning. Never look like automation.
- **The seat count must be real.** Count actual committed founding seats before every Touch 4. When 30 are gone, the offer is gone — that's what makes your *next* deadline believable. No fake timers, ever.
- **STOP means stop.** Any "no", "not interested", "maybe later" gets exactly one line — `Can, no problem. I'm around when you're ready lah 👍` — then mark them in [APP] and never send Touch 5. **The daily videos ARE the long-term follow-up.** They said no to a DM, not to the campaign; Day 21 might close what Day 7 couldn't.
- If someone comments SISTEM again weeks later, don't restart the machine — answer them fresh, like the returning warm lead they are.

Active agents with a real book (roughly 100+ clients) who are visibly drowning in WhatsApp. Not brand-new agents — they have nothing to migrate, no pain, and their "testimonial" proves nothing. The messier their Excel, the better your proof (and your done-for-you migration practice).

# THE LAUNCH PLAN

You do not owe Facebook 60 videos. You owe yourself an answer to one question: *will agents pay for this?* The 60-day storyboard is the library. The launch plan is how you test it without betting two months of your life on hope.

## The 2-Week Test Sprint

Do not commit to 60 days. Commit to 14 — with the 10 strongest self-contained videos, real money behind them, and a written decision rule at the end.

### The 10 test videos — 3 angles

| Angle | Videos (from the storyboard) | What it tests |
|---|---|---|
| **A — Speed-to-lead** | Day 1 "Two Blue Ticks" · Day 13 "Searching for 'Lim'" | Do agents feel the pain of slow replies and lost chats? |
| **B — Lapse-horror** | Day 4 "14 Renewals. Zero Chasing." · Day 5 "The Card That Expired in March" · Day 33 "Three of Your Top Ten Are Red" · Day 49 "One Bounced Payment" · Day 52 "I Didn't Complain. I Just Left." | Does the fear of silent lapses and quiet client exits move them? |
| **C — Time-and-family** | The **Marcus Master** (Days 14+15 merged) · Day 7 "9.47pm, Level 3" · Day 17 "The Empty Chair" | Does "get your evenings back" outpull the money angles? |

### The Marcus Master (one 40-second edit)

Cut Days 14 and 15 into a single video: Marcus's wound (first in, last out, two cases, forehead on the steering wheel) → hard cut on 2 seconds of silence → "Six months later." → the morning list with three names and reasons → close on the thesis line: **"Same person. Different system."** This is your single strongest asset — the whole campaign in 40 seconds. It runs in the sprint AND becomes your evergreen retargeting ad later.

### How the sprint runs

- **Organic:** post Mon / Wed / Fri at 12pm. Six slots in 14 days — lead Week 1 with the strongest of each angle (Day 1, Day 5, Marcus Master), Week 2 with the next tier (Day 13, Day 49, Day 7). The remaining four videos run as paid-only creatives.
- **Paid:** all 10 videos go into Ads Manager from Day 1 as ThruPlay ads — one ad set per angle, **RM30–50/day total** (roughly RM10–15 per angle). This is why you can decide in 14 days instead of 60: paid reach gives you real numbers on every angle fast, instead of waiting for the algorithm to bless you.
- **Every video keeps the standard CTA** on organic: Comment "SISTEM" — I'll DM you the 2-minute demo. (Paid CTAs differ — see Ads Manager Architecture.)
- **Log everything** in the tracking sheet (below) as you go — not at the end.

At Day 14, you sit down with the sheet and apply the gate. Not your feelings. The gate.

## The Day-14 Gate

Write these on a sticky note before Day 1, so Day-14-you can't negotiate with them.

**Definitions:** *3-sec hold* = 3-second video plays ÷ reach. *Comment rate* = SISTEM comments ÷ reach.

- **IF** a video hits **≥30% 3-sec hold** AND **≥1 SISTEM comment per ~300 reach** → it is a **scale candidate**. Move it into the cold ThruPlay campaign as a proven hook, and queue its BM + 中文 versions for next week.
- **IF** a video holds 20–30% but gets almost no SISTEM comments → the hook works, the close doesn't. One retry: re-cut the last 5 seconds (tighter CTA, longer hold on the final frame). If the retry still doesn't comment, retire it.
- **IF two videos from the same angle both come in under 20% hold** → **kill the ANGLE**, not just the videos. The audience is telling you that pain doesn't land with them. No edit fixes an angle nobody feels. Reallocate that angle's budget to the strongest surviving angle.
- **IF you have good holds and comments but zero paying customers by Day 21** → **freeze production. Do not shoot new videos.** The videos are working; the cash register isn't. Fix the offer, the DM close script, and the landing page — then re-run the same proven videos. New creative cannot fix a broken checkout.

### The three possible outcomes at Day 14

1. **Scale** — at least one angle passed and DMs are converting → proceed into the full 60-day storyboard, weighted toward the winning angle, and build out the full ads architecture below.
2. **Pivot** — one angle died, another passed → keep posting, but re-order the storyboard so the winning angle's days come first. You already have 60 scripts; you're just re-sequencing, not re-writing.
3. **Fix the register** — attention but no money → Day-21 rule applies. Freeze, fix the offer and DM flow, re-test with the same creatives for one more week.

## Ads Manager Architecture (not Boost)

The Boost button buys likes. Ads Manager buys the three things this campaign actually needs: cheap video views, retargeting of people who watched, and WhatsApp conversations. Three layers, each feeding the next.

### Layer 1 — COLD: ThruPlay on proven hooks · RM20–30/day

- **Objective:** Video Views (ThruPlay).
- **Creative:** only videos that passed the Day-14 gate. Never put an unproven video on cold traffic.
- **Audience:** Malaysia, 24–50, interest stack around insurance / takaful / financial consulting / MDRT — plus one broad ad set with no interests once you have a lookalike (below). Let the creative do the targeting.
- **Job:** fill the pool of people who have felt the pain. That's all. No offer here.

### Layer 2 — WARM: retarget viewers with the product · RM10–15/day

- **Audience:** custom audience of **50%+ viewers of any video**, 30-day window.
- **Creative:** the demo-style days — Day 4 "14 Renewals. Zero Chasing.", Day 26 "The Dormant 47", Day 49 "One Bounced Payment", Day 57 "The 28, Two Weeks Later" — plus the Marcus Master. They've felt the wound; now show them the actual screen.
- **Switch on** around Day 7–10 of the sprint, once the 50%-viewer pool passes ~1,000 people. Before that it's too small to spend against.

### Layer 3 — HOT: Click-to-WhatsApp with the founding offer · RM10/day

- **Objective:** Messages (Click-to-WhatsApp).
- **Audience:** the warm pool — 50%+ viewers, page/IG engagers, landing-page visitors.
- **Creative:** a plain founder-to-camera or screen ad carrying the founding offer: **RM49/month locked for life, first 30 agents only, includes done-for-you setup of your existing client book** *(recommended pricing — adjust to your economics)*. The seat count is the deadline. Update the number in the ad as seats fill — that's real urgency, the only kind you use.
- **Pre-filled WhatsApp message:** "Hi, I saw the [APP] video — can send me the 2-min demo?" — so the conversation starts itself.

### Monthly ritual — rebuild the lookalike

Once a month, create a fresh **1–3% Malaysia lookalike** seeded from your SISTEM commenters, your WhatsApp-conversation audience, and (once you pass ~100) your customer list. Feed it into Layer 1 as its own ad set. This is how the machine gets smarter every month.

### The side campaign — unit managers

Days 36–39 ("Unit Managers — This One Is for You", "I Caught Her Slump in Week 2", "Recruit 5, Keep 1", "Two Weeks Offline") get their **own small campaign at ~RM10/day**, separate from the agent funnel — aimed at agency leaders (job-title and interest targeting around agency management, plus your own list of unit managers if you have one). Hot layer carries the team offer: **RM39/seat/month for 10+ seats with a group onboarding session** *(recommended — adjust)*. One unit manager is worth 10+ agent sales — this tiny campaign can quietly outperform everything else. Switch it on after the Day-14 gate passes, not before.

### One rule that keeps it all clean

**Comment-gating ("Comment SISTEM") is for organic only.** On paid ads, never ask for a comment — paid clicks go straight to WhatsApp. You're paying for the click; don't add a detour.

## The Tracking Sheet

One Google Sheet, one tab, one row per video per week. If a number isn't in this sheet, it doesn't exist.

| Column | What goes in it |
|---|---|
| **Date** | Date posted (or week of, for ads) |
| **Video** | Day # + name + angle (e.g. "D5 Card Expired — Lapse") |
| **Reach** | From FB insights / Ads Manager |
| **3s hold %** | 3-second plays ÷ reach |
| **SISTEM comments** | Count of keyword comments |
| **DMs started** | People who actually got/opened the demo DM |
| **Demos watched** | Clicks on the demo link — use a UTM per video (`?utm_source=fb&utm_content=d05`) or one short link per video so you know which video sent them |
| **Trials / setups** | People who said yes to "want me to set it up for you?" |
| **Paid** | Paying customers attributed to that video/conversation |
| **Notes** | One line: what you noticed, what you changed |

Each column should be a step down from the one before it. Where the numbers fall off a cliff between two adjacent columns — that's your leak, and that's the only thing you work on next week.

### The weekly 15-minute review (Sunday night, before batch-production)

1. **(5 min)** Fill in the week's numbers for every row.
2. **(5 min)** Answer three questions in writing: Which video held best? Which converted comments → DMs best? Where is the biggest drop-off between adjacent columns?
3. **(4 min)** Make exactly two decisions: which video gets next week's budget (and which gets killed), and which winner gets its BM + 中文 version made this week.
4. **(1 min)** One line in Notes. Done. Close the laptop and go make the week's videos with those decisions — not with vibes.

## Cadence, Cross-Posting & Language

### Cadence: 3 per week, not 7

**Monday / Wednesday / Friday, 12pm** — the lunch-hour scroll, when agents are between appointments with their phone in hand. Three good videos beat seven rushed ones, and 3×/week turns the 60-script storyboard into ~20 weeks of material instead of a 60-day death march. Keep the Sunday batch-production ritual from the storyboard — at 3/week it drops to roughly 90 minutes.

### Same-day cross-posting (one master, four platforms)

Every master goes out the same day to TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts. Export clean (no platform watermarks — a TikTok logo on a Reel quietly kills its reach). Adjust only the CTA:

| Platform | CTA |
|---|---|
| **Facebook** | Comment "SISTEM" — I'll DM you the 2-minute demo (comment automation) |
| **Instagram Reels** | Same — Comment "SISTEM" (IG comment automation works via ManyChat/Chatrace) |
| **TikTok** | "2-min demo — link in bio" (bio link → landing page; comment automation is unreliable here) |
| **YouTube Shorts** | "Demo link in the description" + pin a comment with the link (no DM automation exists) |

You are not "doing four platforms." You are doing one video and pressing upload three extra times.

### Language: winners get translated weekly, not at Day 60

Each week's **winner** (by the gate metrics) gets its **BM and 中文 versions shipped the following week** — same edit, re-recorded voiceover, re-done text overlays. Do not sit on a proven video in one language for two months while the Malay- and Chinese-first halves of your market scroll past. Where the storyboard already flags a day as BM/中文 priority (Days 1, 4, 5, 17…), those overlap with your likely winners anyway.

### Sunday

Repost the week's best performer as a Story with the poll: "Which one are you — the WhatsApp scroller or the system agent?" Costs nothing, keeps the warm audience warm, and the poll answers are a list of self-identified prospects.

## Owned-Audience Backstop

Everything above lives on rented land. One algorithm change, one ad-account review, one page restriction — and the machine stops. Build these two things in week one so the business survives Facebook.

### 1. A WhatsApp Channel (plus your Business number)

Create a **[APP] WhatsApp Channel** and invite everyone who ever DMs you — end every demo conversation with "I post one client-saving tip a week here, join lah: [channel link]." Post 2–3 times a week: the week's video, one practical tip, and honest build-in-public updates (seat count of the founding 30 included). A Channel is broadcast-only — no group noise, no admin burden — and it's a list Facebook can never take from you. Malaysian agents live in WhatsApp; meet the list where it already is.

### 2. A one-page landing site

One page. Three things, in this order:

1. **The 2-minute demo video** at the top.
2. **Pricing, in plain sight** — RM79/month or RM790/year · founding member RM49/month locked for life, first 30 agents, with done-for-you setup of your existing client book · teams RM39/seat/month for 10+ seats with group onboarding *(recommended — adjust to your economics)*. Showing the price filters out tyre-kickers before they reach your inbox.
3. **One green WhatsApp button.** No sign-up form, no email capture, no chatbot widget. In this market the form IS WhatsApp.

This page is where every off-Facebook CTA points (TikTok bio, YouTube description), and it's the asset that still works the day an ad account doesn't.

### Pixel / CAPI note (5-minute job, do it before the first ad)

Install the **Meta Pixel** on the landing page on day one, and set up the **Conversions API** if your page host supports it (iOS under-reports pixel-only events). Fire two events: a custom **demo_watched** (demo video played) and **Contact** (WhatsApp button clicked). These two events are what let you retarget demo-watchers who didn't message, and they fatten the seed audience for your monthly lookalike. Ads Manager also builds audiences from your Click-to-WhatsApp conversations automatically — between the pixel and those, your warm and hot layers keep filling themselves.

**The standing rule:** every conversation ends with the person somewhere you own — a WhatsApp thread, the Channel, or a row in your customer list. Facebook's job is only to introduce you.
